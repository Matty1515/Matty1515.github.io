European Qualification

The top six teams qualify for next season's European Rugby Champions Cup and the team that finishes in 7th place will advance to a playoff round where they can compete for a place. Teams that haven't qualified for the Champions Cup will play in next season's European Rugby Challenge Cup instead.

Relegation

The club at the bottom of the table after the regular season ends is relegated to the RFU Championship. They will be replaced by the winner of Championship assuming the promoted club meets the Minimum Standards Criteria. If the criteria is not met then no promotion/relegation will take place.

On April 7th 2018, Bristol Rugby (now Bristol Bears) secured promotion to the Aviva Premiership.

Final

At the end of the season, the top 4 teams enter the playoff semi-finals. The 1st place team will host the 4th, and the 2nd will host the 3rd. The winners of the semi-finals will then play each other at Twickenham Stadium with the victor being crowned Premiership Champions. The final ranking this year is: #1 Exeter, #2 Saracens, #3 Wasps, #4 Newcastle.

After Saracen's 57-33 victory against Wasps and Exeter's 36-5 win against Newcastle, the grand final of the 17/18 season has been set. On the 26th of May, Exeter will face Saracens with the winner being crowned the premiership rugby champion.
