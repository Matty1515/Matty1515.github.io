Gallagher Premiership Fixtures & Results


The top level competition for rugby union clubs in England is the Gallagher Premiership. The league consists of twelve teams with playoffs, European qualification, promotion and relegation to all be played for. The competition started in 1987 with the most successful side being Leicester Tigers with 10 titles. It is currently the 18/19 season (played from 31 August 2018 to the 25 May 2019) as last year's winners Saracens look to defend their title.

Teams are awarded league points depending on the result of the match:

  • 4 points for a win
  • 2 points for a draw
  • 0 points for a loss

Teams can also be awarded bonus points if they meet certain criteria in a match:

  • 1 point for a team that loses by 7 points or less
  • 1 point for a team that scores 4 or more tries

At the end of the season, the top 4 teams enter the playoff semi-finals. The 1st place team will host the 4th, and the 2nd will host the 3rd. The winners of the semi-finals will then play each other at Twickenham Stadium with the victor being crowned Premiership Champions.

European Qualification

The top six teams qualify for next season's European Rugby Champions Cup and the team that finishes in 7th place will advance to a playoff round where they can compete for a place. Teams that haven't qualified for the Champions Cup will play in next season's European Rugby Challenge Cup instead.

Relegation

After the regular season concludes, the club at the bottom of the table is relegated to the RFU Championship. They will be replaced by the winner of Championship assuming the promoted club meets the Minimum Standards Criteria. If the criteria is not met then no promotion/relegation will take place.
