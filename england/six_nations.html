Six Nations


England results & fixtures at the Six Nations


The original Home Nations Championship (the first international rugby union tournament) was played between teams from England, Ireland, Scotland and Wales. In 1940 this became the Five Nations Championship with the addition of France, which in turn became the Six Nations Championship in 2000 when Italy joined the competition. The record winners of the Home Nations, Five Nations and Six Nations tournaments, is England with 28 outright titles (and an additional 10 shared titles), Wales follow closely with 26 (12) wins and France are 3rd with 17 (8) wins.

A Grand Slam is awarded when a team wins all of its games and the wooden spoon is "awarded" to the team that finished last. England have won the most Grand Slams with 13 titles in total.

Last 3 Years

England Grand Slam

On the 13th of March, England won their first Six Nations championship since 2011 with a game to spare. Six days later they completed their thirteenth Grand Slam with a win over France in Paris. Italy, on the other hand, were record losers failing to win a single game and conceding a record 29 tries.

England Win

For the second year in a row England won the Six Nations championship before playing their final game. However, they were not able to complete the Grand Slam this time due to a 13-9 defeat to Ireland on the final day. This tournament also saw the introduction of the bonus point system, rewarding teams for scoring more tries thereby pushing the tournament into a more attacking climate.

Ireland Grand Slam

After four wins from four games, Ireland won the Six Nations championship on the 10th of March 2018. A week later, on St. Patrick's Days, Ireland beat England 24-15 to secure their third ever Grand Slam.


Six Nations 2019

The 2019 Six Nations Championship will be the 20th series of the Six Nations, the 125th edition of the competition altogether. England will look to bounce back after a poor 2018 campaign and gain some momentum going into the Rugby World Cup later in the year.

Teams are awarded league points depending on the result of the match:

  • 4 points for a win
  • 2 points for a draw
  • 0 points for a loss

Teams can also be awarded bonus points if they meet certain criteria in a match:

  • 1 point for a team that loses by 7 points or less
  • 1 point for a team that scores 4 or more tries

The Grand Slam winner, should there be one, will be awarded an additional 3 points to make sure that they finish top of the table.
